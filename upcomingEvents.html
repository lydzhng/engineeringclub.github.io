SCIENCE OLYMPICS

The Engineering Club participates in the annual Science Olympics,
which includes a variety of challenges from different science disciplines,
including physics, engineering, biology, computer science, and math.
The club works together in teams to participate in challenges and is awarded
points based on their performance. The winners are announced at the end of the challenge.

EXTERNAL EVENTS

Participating in a cardboard boat race can be a fun and
engaging experience for high school students. It provides
an opportunity to combine teamwork with design and construction skills,
while also allowing students to showcase their creativity and technical
skills. Additionally, it can help to increase workplace campaigns and raise
awareness about the importance of working together.


Competing in the Wonderland Wondercoaster competition can provide similar benefits,
as it also requires students to work as a team and use their creative and technical skills.
It can also be a great way for students to have fun and make new friends, while also learning
about different areas of engineering.

CLUB EVENTS

Engineering club participates in a range of events, including technical talks,
competitions, and interactive activities. These events are free to attend and
provide insight into different areas of engineering, as well as the opportunity
to meet other students and professionals. The club also hosted a design competition
during winter, where students were tasked with building a gingerbread house in teams
of up to 3. This competition allowed students to showcase their creative thinking and
technical skills, while also providing a fun, festive and interactive learning experience.
