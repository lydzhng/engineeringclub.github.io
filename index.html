About the RHHS Engineering Club

The Engineering Club is a group of students who are interested in engineering technology and its application to the real world. The club aims to encourage an active engagement in the engineering field above and beyond the experience of the classroom. Club members participate in solving problems, creating solutions for everyday problems, and designing, planning and constructing different projects. The club is open to all levels of students who are interested in science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM).

What we do

High school engineering clubs provide an opportunity for students to explore engineering and technology, and to enhance their skills in problem-solving, teamwork, and cooperation. The clubs aim to improve students' competitiveness for college applications and job resumes. Students will have many opportunities to design, plan, and construct different projects. These projects are usually completed in groups, allowing students to work together and utilize their math and science skills to solve real-world problems. Often, engineering clubs will participate in competitions involving problem-solving and creativity. Some competitions involve tasks that test students' skills in building and designing structures.
